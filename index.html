Our Heritage & Craft

Clyde & Conrad Honey Co. began as a passion project fueled by a deep respect for the remarkable honeybee. Nestled in the rolling green landscapes of North Oxfordshire, our hives sit amidst the ancient hedgerows and wildflower meadows of Adderbury. We don't just "keep" bees; we steward them, ensuring they have a diverse and healthy environment to thrive in year-round.

Unlike commercial honey producers, we embrace a "slow honey" movement. We follow the rhythm of the seasons, allowing our bees to forage naturally on the local nectar of hawthorn, chestnut, and summer wildflowers. This connection to the land results in a harvest that changes subtly in flavor and color with every passing year—a true vintage of the Adderbury countryside.

Our philosophy is simple: interfere as little as possible. We take only the surplus honey that the bees don't need for winter, ensuring the colony remains strong and resilient. Every jar you hold is a reflection of our commitment to sustainable, ethical beekeeping.

The Power of Raw Honey

Most supermarket honey is ultra-filtered and pasteurized at high temperatures to maintain a consistent liquid state. This process kills the live enzymes, destroys the delicate aromatic compounds, and removes the beneficial local pollen that makes honey a true superfood.

At Clyde & Conrad, we believe that nature got the recipe right the first time. Our honey is raw—meaning it has never been heated above the natural temperature of the beehive. This preserves the incredible complexity of its flavor profile and keeps its health-boosting properties intact.

When you taste our honey, you aren't just tasting sweetness; you are tasting the minerals, antioxidants, and floral notes of the Adderbury terroir. It is honey in its most honest, potent, and delicious form.
